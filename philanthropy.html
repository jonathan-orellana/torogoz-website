Our Philanthropy

Service begins with understanding the needs of the community around us — and acting on them with purpose.

Service with purpose

The Torogoz Chapter believes that service begins with understanding the needs of the community around us. Through intentional programming, partnership, and hands-on work, our brothers seek to make a meaningful impact at the University of Virginia and beyond.

Our approach to philanthropy is rooted in brotherhood, cultural awareness, and leadership. We support causes that reflect our values, strengthen our communities, and create opportunities for brothers to serve with purpose.

National Philanthropy

The fight against HIV / AIDS

Lambda Sigma Upsilon's national philanthropy is the fight against HIV/AIDS — a cause that lives at the heart of our brotherhood. It honors founding father Alberto Rivera, who passed from AIDS-related complications in 1989.

Through education, awareness, and fundraising, our chapter works to break stigma, support those affected, and carry his memory forward — turning loss into a lasting commitment to our communities.

Chapter Philanthropy

Rooted in our local community

As a new chapter, we're in the process of building lasting partnerships within the Charlottesville community. Our chapter philanthropy is centered on supporting and bringing awareness to local Latino communities — with a focus on immigration-related issues and the resources families need to thrive.

By working alongside local organizations whose missions align with our own, we hope to turn brotherhood into real, lasting impact close to home.
